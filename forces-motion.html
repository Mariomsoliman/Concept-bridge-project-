What is force?

A force is a push or a pull on an object. Forces can start motion, stop motion, or change how something is moving.

What is motion?

Motion is a change in position over time. To describe motion, we talk about how fast an object moves and in what direction.

Types of forces

There are many kinds of forces, such as gravity, friction, normal force, and applied forces. Each type affects motion in a slightly different way.

Net force

When more than one force acts on an object, they combine to create a net force. The net force determines how the object's motion will change.

Real-world examples

From a ball rolling down a hill to a car stopping at a red light, forces and motion are at work everywhere in everyday life.

Connection to Computer Science

Computer simulations and video games use rules about forces and motion to move objects on the screen. Programs update positions step by step, similar to how physics describes motion over time.
